    Understanding Your Stress Level

    Stress is your body's natural response to challenges and demands. While short-term stress can boost focus and performance, chronic stress affects your physical health, mental well-being, and daily productivity. This free stress self-assessment measures your perceived stress across 5 key life areas: work/study, relationships, health, finance, and daily life. By identifying which areas cause the most tension, you can take targeted action to restore balance.

    Tips to Manage Stress

    • Exercise regularly -- even a 20-minute walk can lower cortisol levels and improve mood.
    • Practice deep breathing or meditation for 5-10 minutes daily to activate your relaxation response.
    • Prioritize sleep -- aim for 7-8 hours of quality rest each night.
    • Set healthy boundaries at work and in relationships to prevent burnout.
    • Talk to someone you trust or seek professional counseling when stress feels overwhelming.
